Our Approach

Education that empowers, not overwhelms

Our courses are small, practical, and personal. We don't believe in passive learning — every session is hands-on, accessible, and designed to give you something you can use immediately.

Whether you're just starting out or looking to deepen knowledge you already have, there is a place for you here. We teach from experience, not just theory.

What We Teach

Our Short Courses

Practical, approachable workshops for individuals and families. All courses are available in small groups — enquire for upcoming dates.

Land & Growing

Introduction to Permaculture

Discover the principles of permaculture design — working with nature rather than against it. This course covers observation, design thinking, soil health, food growing, and how to apply permaculture ethics to everyday life and land. Suitable for beginners and those with gardens of any size.

Enquire about dates →
Fermentation & Nutrition

Kefir & Fermented Foods

Explore the world of live fermented foods — from milk and water kefir to kombucha, sauerkraut, and beyond. Learn the science of fermentation, the health benefits of a diverse microbiome, and how to make and maintain your own cultures at home with confidence.

Enquire about dates →
Bread & Baking

Sourdough Bread Making

From starter to loaf — a hands-on sourdough workshop covering how to build and maintain a healthy starter, develop dough, shape, score, and bake a proper sourdough loaf at home. No special equipment needed. You'll leave with your own starter to take home.

Enquire about dates →
Family & Parenting

Home Education & Parenting Guidance

For families considering or already practising home education. We offer practical guidance on getting started, structuring learning, finding community, and nurturing children who are curious, confident, and self-directed. Grounded in values-led, child-centred approaches.

Enquire about dates →
Urban Homesteading

Keeping Chickens in the City

Think you can't keep chickens because you live in a town or city? Think again. This practical workshop covers everything you need to know — choosing breeds, housing, feeding, health, egg management, and how to keep happy hens in a smaller urban space. Families welcome.
